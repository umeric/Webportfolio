Project 2 | Depression and Anxiety Based on Reported Frequency of Symptoms During Last 7 Days

Resource: https://data.cdc.gov/NCHS/Indicators-of-Anxiety-or-Depression-Based-on-Repor/8pt5-q6wp

1. What is the average anxiety or depression level for each race/ethnicity?
The data reveals varying anxiety and depressive symptom percentages across different racial and ethnic groups. "Non-Hispanic, other races and multiple races" have the highest anxiety level (38.94%), while "Hispanic or Latino" follow closely (33.91%). Meanwhile, "Non-Hispanic Asian, single race" individuals show the lowest depressive symptoms (19.64%).

SELECT indicator, subgroup AS race_ethnicity, CONCAT(CAST(AVG(value) AS DECIMAL(10,2)), '%') AS average_anxiety_depression_level_percentage FROM depress WHERE indicator IN ('Symptoms of Anxiety Disorder', 'Symptoms of Depressive Disorder') AND "group" = 'By Race/Hispanic ethnicity' GROUP BY indicator, subgroup ORDER BY race_ethnicity;

View Query
2. What is the average depression level over the years for the Symptoms of Depressive Disorder?
Over the years, the average depression level for the Symptoms of Depressive Disorder has shown a gradual reduction, with some slight fluctuations in certain years. In 2020, the average depression level was 25.65, which decreased to 23.73 in 2021. It slightly increased to 23.78 in 2022 and further decreased to 22.56 in 2023.

SELECT TO_CHAR(time_period_start_date, 'YYYY') AS year, CAST(AVG(value) AS numeric(10, 2)) AS average_depression_level FROM depress WHERE indicator = 'Symptoms of Depressive Disorder' GROUP BY year ORDER BY year;

3. What are the top 5 states with the highest anxiety levels?
The top 5 states with the highest anxiety levels are Louisiana, Oklahoma, Nevada, West Virginia, and Mississippi.

SELECT state, CAST(AVG(value) AS DECIMAL(10, 2)) AS average_anxiety_level FROM depress WHERE indicator = 'Symptoms of Anxiety Disorder' AND "group" = 'By State' GROUP BY state ORDER BY average_anxiety_level DESC LIMIT 5;

4. Are anxiety and depression levels different across age groups?
Anxiety and depression levels vary across age groups. The highest levels are observed in the "18 - 29 years" age group, gradually decreasing with older age groups. The lowest levels are recorded in the "80 years and above" age group.

SELECT d1.subgroup AS age_group, CAST(AVG(d1.value) AS DECIMAL(10, 2)) AS average_anxiety_level, CAST(AVG(d2.value) AS DECIMAL(10, 2)) AS average_depression_level FROM depress d1 JOIN depress d2 ON d1.subgroup = d2.subgroup WHERE d1.indicator = 'Symptoms of Anxiety Disorder' AND d2.indicator = 'Symptoms of Depressive Disorder' AND d1."group" = 'By Age' AND d2."group" = 'By Age' GROUP BY d1.subgroup;
